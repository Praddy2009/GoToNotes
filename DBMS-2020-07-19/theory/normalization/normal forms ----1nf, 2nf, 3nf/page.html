Normal forms
It provides a stepwise progression towards the construction of normalized relation schemas, which are free from data redundancies.

First Normal form (1NF)
  • A relation will be 1NF if it contains an atomic value
  • It states that an attribute of a table can't hold multiple values. It must hold only single valued attribute.
  • It disallows : Multivalued attribute, composite attribute and their combination.
  • Steps to make it 1NF form
  • We remove the repeating groups by
    • Method 1:Flatten the table and change the key
    • Method 2: Decomposition the relation

Second Normal form (2NF)
A relation is 2NF iff 2 conditions are satisfied simultaniously:
  • It's in 1NF.
  • No non key attribute is partially dependent on any key.
    i.e., no non-prime attribute (attributes which are not part of any candidate key) is dependent on any proper subset of any candidate key of the table.
